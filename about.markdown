## NNU Aerospace Club

NNU Aerospace is a student led club at Northwest Nazarene University in Nampa, ID. NNU has been dabbling in aerospace projects since 2007 or so. In the beginning, projects were confined to NASA's RockON program. RockOn was a 5 day workshop where students got to put a small payload together from a kit and watch it launch on a Terrier-Malamute Improved sounding rocket. 

 After a few years of that program, NNU continued to the RockSat-C program, which involved building an experiment that fit in a 16" diamater volume 5 inches high. Each experiment recieved power and telemetry lines from on-rocket batteries. The entire capsule was pressurised, so it didn't experience the full force of the vacuum of space, re-entry, or saltwater corrosion after splashdown. The design process for this project involved a year long review process, with intial, interim, and full simulation reviews.
 
 Three cubesat projects were completed by NNU in the late 2010's. One satellite is still in orbit as of July 2026. 
 
 After several years of RockSat-C, NNU graduated to RockSat-X, the same as C except the capsule isn't pressurized. Thus the project experiences the full brunt of space, re-entry, and the ocean. 
 
 As of 2026, RockSat-C has been dropped, so RockSat-X is now refered to simply as RockSat
